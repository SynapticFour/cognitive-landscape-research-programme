# CLRP-008: Cultural Adaptation

## Abstract

Cross-locale work requires **adaptation**, not mere translation. CLRP defines principles for ecological validity, local participation, and honest reporting of normative data.

## 1. Adaptation ≠ translation

| Translation | Adaptation |
|-------------|------------|
| Linguistic equivalence focus | Construct and experiential equivalence |
| Often single expert | Participatory local input |
| Assumes source locale universal | Tests applicability in target locale |

Both may be necessary; neither alone sufficient.

## 2. Principles

### C1 — Local participation

Item development and review includes people embedded in target locale contexts.

### C2 — Separate validation tracks

Each locale requires its own validation evidence (CLRP-006) before normative claims.

### C3 — No silent comparability

Cross-locale score comparison requires measurement invariance evidence. Until then, describe scores as **locale-relative**.

### C4 — Cultural humility

Absence of evidence is not evidence of absence. Failed adaptation is reported.

### C5 — Attribution

Adapted materials credit source programme (CC BY 4.0) and document changes.

## 3. Locale descriptor

Minimum metadata:

- Region / community context
- Language(s)
- Adaptation team and date
- Relationship to source instrument version

## 4. Workstream model

CLRP recognises parallel workstreams:

- **A — Psychometric validation** (CLRP-VR)
- **B — Cultural adaptation** (documented in validation or CLRP-TR)

Neither substitutes for the other.

## 5. Non-goals

- Defining specific translation workflows
- Mandating a single locale taxonomy
- Claiming CLRP resolves colonial legacies in psychometrics (ongoing responsibility)
